Discover the Flavors of Karachi: A Foodie's Paradise

Travel 1/16/2026

Karachi, the largest city in Pakistan, is a melting pot of cultures and cuisines. The city's street food scene is a reflection of its rich history and diversity, with a wide range of traditional and modern dishes to try. From spicy kebabs to sweet desserts, Karachi's street food is a culinary adventure waiting to happen.

One of the most popular street foods in Karachi is the kebab. Made from marinated meat cooked on a skewer, kebabs are often served with a side of chutney or salad. You can find kebab vendors on almost every street corner, especially in the old city area. Another popular dish is the bun kebab, a type of kebab served in a bun with vegetables and sauce.

Karachi's street food scene is not just about kebabs and other meat-based dishes. The city is also famous for its sweet treats, such as falooda and kulfi. Falooda is a cold dessert made from vermicelli noodles, milk, and sugar, while kulfi is a type of Indian-style ice cream. You can find vendors selling these desserts at markets and street food stalls throughout the city.

In addition to traditional street foods, Karachi is also home to a number of modern food trucks and cafes. These establishments offer a range of international cuisines, from Italian to Mexican, as well as local specialties like nihari and haleem. You can find food trucks and cafes in popular areas like Clifton and DHA.

For a truly authentic experience, visit the Empress Market in the old city area. This bustling market is home to a wide range of street food vendors, from kebab sellers to falooda vendors. You can also find fresh produce, spices, and other local goods at the market. Be sure to try some of the local specialties, such as the famous Karachi-style biryani.
